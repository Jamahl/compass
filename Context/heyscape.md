## What They Do

Heyscape is a Perth-based eco-tourism startup offering off-grid tiny cabin experiences across Western Australia. They bridge the gap between comfort and nature — giving people access to remote, premium locations that would otherwise be inaccessible, without sacrificing the amenities that make a stay enjoyable.

**Core product and experience:**

- **Off-grid tiny cabins** — 25 thoughtfully designed cabins nestled in secluded natural locations across WA, from the Perth Hills and Avon Valley to Margaret River, Busselton, Yallingup, and Denmark
- **Eco-friendly design** — solar panels, composting toilets, potable tank water, biodegradable products, and energy-conscious amenities throughout
- **Premium comfort** — queen/single beds, full linen, kitchenette, mini fridge, outdoor BBQ, hammocks, reverse-cycle air conditioning, and USB charging points
- **Diverse stay types** — couples' retreats, family cabins, dog-friendly stays, and winery/farm experiences
- **Gifting** — branded gift vouchers positioned as a premium gifting experience for rest and reconnection
- **Landowner partnerships** — Heyscape leases land from rural and regional WA landowners (farms, vineyards, properties bordering national parks), handles all development, builds and fits cabins locally, and manages bookings and guest experience end-to-end
- **Landmark activations** — in 2024, Heyscape ran a trial of three tiny cabins inside Kings Park, Perth, showcasing WA as a tourism destination

A minimum two-night stay is required at all locations (three nights on long weekends), reinforcing the slow travel and disconnection philosophy at the heart of the brand.

## Who Their Customers Are

**Primary customers:** Western Australian residents and interstate visitors seeking a premium nature escape without roughing it.

**Key customer profiles:**
- Couples looking for a romantic, secluded retreat
- Families wanting an immersive nature experience for children
- Dog owners — Heyscape offers pet-friendly cabin options
- Corporates and gifting buyers — gift vouchers are an active revenue channel
- Wellness-focused travellers seeking digital detox and mindful slow travel
- Interstate visitors using direct flights to Busselton or Margaret River as a gateway

**Geographic reach:**
- All locations within Western Australia
- Cabins spread across Perth Hills, Avon Valley, Margaret River region, Busselton/Yallingup, and Denmark
- Guests travel from Perth metro and interstate (Sydney–Busselton direct flights open up the South West market)

## Founder Background

**Pierre Sauvignon — Founder (departed)**

Pierre founded Heyscape in 2019 with a clear vision: make premium off-grid nature experiences accessible to everyday West Australians. He built the business from inside BetterLabs' venture studio, leveraging the RAC WA network and backing to establish the cabin portfolio and landowner partnership model. Pierre's personal values around sustainability, slow travel, and connection to WA's natural environment are embedded in every aspect of the Heyscape brand. He has since left Heyscape to focus on his family.

**Tim Brady — Head of Heyscape**

Tim Brady joined Heyscape following Pierre's departure, bringing commercial and operational experience (CPA Australia background) to scale the business. He has been publicly associated with the Kings Park activation milestone and has been positioning Heyscape for its next phase of growth. On LinkedIn, Tim speaks to the challenges of founder-led businesses scaling beyond their founders — a theme directly relevant to his own role.

**Origin Story**

Heyscape was born from a recognition that Western Australia's most beautiful, remote landscapes were largely inaccessible to everyday travellers without sacrificing comfort. Pierre saw an opportunity to build a sustainable, design-led cabin network that respected the land and its custodians while creating genuine economic value for rural landowners. Starting in 2019 and building through the pandemic period — when the appetite for local, nature-based travel surged — Heyscape grew to 25 cabins across WA, with a model that scales by adding new landowner partnerships rather than purchasing land outright.

## Competitors & Market Position

Heyscape operates in the WA eco-tourism and unique stays market, competing with both traditional accommodation providers and emerging experience-led platforms.

| Company | What They Do | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Airbnb / Stayz** | Short-term rental platforms with listings across WA including unique/rural stays | Indirect competitor — Heyscape is a curated brand vs. a marketplace |
| **Glamping Hub** | Global glamping and unique stays booking platform | Direct competitor in the glamping/off-grid category |
| **Discovery Parks** | Large-scale holiday park network across Australia | Competitor at the family/nature stays end of the market |
| **Roam Beyond** | Premium glamping experiences in WA (Kimberley focus) | Direct competitor in premium WA eco-experiences |
| **Margaret River region B&Bs and retreats** | Established boutique accommodation across the South West | Local competitor in the couples/retreat market |
| **Unyoked** | Off-grid cabin network across Australia's east coast | Closest national competitor — similar model and brand positioning |

**Heyscape's market position:** Heyscape occupies the premium end of the WA eco-stay market — above budget camping, below luxury lodges. Their closest national competitor is Unyoked (east coast focused), which validates the model but also signals what Heyscape could face if Unyoked expands westward. Heyscape's differentiation is its deep WA identity, landowner partnership model, and BetterLabs institutional backing — which gives it both brand credibility and operational support beyond what a bootstrapped competitor could access.

## Pain Points

- **Geographic concentration** — all cabins are in WA; any downturn in WA domestic tourism directly impacts the business
- **Seasonal demand** — nature-based stays are weather-dependent; fire ban seasons, summer heat, and extreme weather limit bookings in some locations
- **Land and development lead time** — each new location requires finding suitable land, development approvals, local shire engagement, and cabin construction, all of which take significant time and capital
- **Founder transition risk** — Pierre's departure creates a culture and brand continuity challenge that Tim Brady is now navigating
- **Occupancy pressure** — a growing cabin portfolio requires consistent occupancy to cover fixed costs of land lease, maintenance, and staffing
- **Competition from Airbnb/Stayz** — individual landowners can list unique stays independently, without needing Heyscape as an intermediary
- **Scaling complexity** — unlike software, each new cabin is a physical asset that must be built, maintained, and managed

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths

- Unique, design-led brand with strong WA identity and community following
- Proven landowner partnership model — scalable without purchasing land
- BetterLabs / RAC WA backing provides institutional credibility and operational support
- 25 cabins across diverse WA locations offers variety and repeat visit potential
- Strong gifting and corporate channel with high-margin gift voucher product
- Eco-friendly and sustainability credentials resonate with the target market
- Kings Park activation demonstrated ability to win high-profile institutional partnerships
- Minimum two-night stay policy drives higher revenue per booking and deeper guest experience

### Weaknesses

- All cabins in WA — limited geographic diversification
- Physical cabin model is capital-intensive and slow to scale vs. digital businesses
- Founder departure creates short-term culture and brand continuity risk
- Seasonal demand fluctuations and weather dependency
- Relatively small team managing a growing physical asset portfolio

### Opportunities

- Domestic tourism boom post-COVID has elevated appetite for unique, local nature experiences
- Direct flights to Busselton/Margaret River open the South West to interstate visitors
- Expanding the landowner partnership model into new WA regions (Kimberley, Pilbara, Great Southern)
- Corporate retreats and team experience packages — an underserved B2B revenue stream
- International tourism recovery — WA is increasingly on the radar for UK and European travellers
- Partnership with RAC member base for exclusive offers and discounts (mirroring Strata Town model)
- Potential to white-label or franchise the model to other Australian states

### Threats

- Unyoked or east coast glamping operators entering the WA market
- Individual landowners listing unique stays directly on Airbnb, bypassing Heyscape
- Bushfire seasons and extreme weather events disrupting booking windows
- Economic pressure on discretionary travel spending
- Rising construction costs impacting cabin build economics
- Regulatory changes to short-stay accommodation or rural development approvals in WA
