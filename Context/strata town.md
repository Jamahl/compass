# Strata Town — Company Analysis

**Date:** April 2026
**Prepared by:** Leya Wall, Co-Founder & Head of Clients, Town Square

---

## What They Do

Strata Town is a Perth-based strata management company operating across Western Australia. They position themselves as a technology-forward alternative to traditional strata managers, combining experienced local staff with their proprietary Town Square platform to deliver a more transparent and responsive service.

**Core service capabilities:**

- **Residential strata management** — apartments, townhouses, villas, and duplexes across all Perth suburbs
- **Commercial strata management** — retail and commercial strata schemes
- **Mixed-use strata management** — complexes combining residential and commercial occupants
- **New development strata management** — by-law recommendations, budget and levy calculations, handover support for developers
- **Strata consulting** — financial audits, legal documentation, by-law drafting, dispute resolution
- **Town Square portal** — their client-facing platform giving owners and committee members 24/7 access to financials, scheme documents, open tickets, and real-time status updates
- **AGM facilitation** — onsite AGM facilities and high-quality video AGM capabilities

Strata Town differentiates on three main points: no insurance commissions added above the base premium, flexible rolling 30-day contracts for new clients, and an RAC member benefit offering 15% off management fees quarterly.

---

## Who Their Customers Are

**Primary customers:** Lot owners, councils of owners (committees), and property developers across Perth, WA.

**Scheme types served:**
- Residential (apartments, townhouses, villas, duplexes)
- Commercial (retail and office strata)
- Mixed-use (combined residential and commercial)
- New developments (pre-settlement and handover)

**Key decision-makers:**
- Council of Owners / committee members who appoint and manage the strata manager relationship
- Individual lot owners seeking a switch from an underperforming manager
- Property developers requiring strata setup and handover services

**Geographic focus:** Perth metro and surrounding suburbs. WA-only at this stage.

## Founder Background

**Clinton House — Founder (departed)**

Clinton House founded Strata Town with a clear technology-first vision for the strata management industry. In public interviews, he described Strata Town as (to his knowledge) the only strata management company in WA to have built a custom software platform from the ground up specifically for owners and councils. That platform — Town Square — was designed to streamline workflows, educate owners, and keep all stakeholders informed in real time. Clinton has since left the industry.

**Andrew Kirkwood — General Manager**

Andrew Kirkwood currently leads Strata Town as General Manager, overseeing day-to-day operations and service delivery across the portfolio.

**RAC WA — Parent Organisation**

Strata Town is 100% owned by RAC WA, one of Western Australia's most recognised and trusted organisations. This ownership provides Strata Town with brand credibility, a built-in member distribution channel (15% discount for RAC members), and institutional stability — an unusual advantage for a relatively young strata management company.

**Origin Story**

Strata Town was founded as a fresh alternative to the incumbent strata management experience in WA — one that prioritised technology, transparency, and rolling contracts over the lock-in and opacity that characterised the traditional industry. The Town Square platform was built internally as a core part of this vision and has since been spun out as its own independent business (the Town Square that this document's author co-founded).

## Competitors & Market Position

Strata Town operates in the WA strata management services market — a fragmented space with a mix of large national players and smaller boutique operators.

| Company | What They Do | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Rezzi** | Perth-based boutique strata management firm; led by Nikki D'Agostino | Direct competitor in Perth market |
| **Bright & Duggan** | National strata management group with WA presence | Larger incumbent competitor |
| **PICA Group** | One of Australia's largest strata management companies | National competitor |
| **Realmark Strata** | Perth-based strata management division of Realmark | Local competitor |
| **SVN Strata** | Perth-based strata and commercial property management | Local competitor |
| **Town Square (platform)** | Communications platform — originally built by Strata Town, now independent | Technology partner / vendor |

**Strata Town's market position:** A technology-differentiated boutique operator in a market dominated by large, traditional players. Their RAC WA ownership provides credibility and distribution that most small strata companies don't have. The spin-out of Town Square as an independent platform is an interesting signal — it suggests the technology has value beyond internal use and was commercially validated first inside Strata Town.

## Pain Points

- **Scale constraints** — small team of ~7 limits growth capacity without proportional hiring
- **WA-only footprint** — no national presence limits growth ceiling compared to PICA and Bright & Duggan
- **Technology dependency** — now relies on Town Square (an external vendor) for their core client portal, having spun it out
- **Price sensitivity** — the 15% RAC rebate and no-commission insurance model are compelling but compress margins
- **Switching friction** — despite flexible contracts, inertia in strata management is high; council decisions require formal votes
- **Perception gap** — being RAC-owned could feel corporate to owners looking for a boutique feel

## Red Flags & Risks

| Risk | Detail |
|---|---|
| **External technology dependency** | Town Square is now an independent company — Strata Town relies on a third party for their core client-facing platform |
| **Small team** | ~7 employees limits service capacity and creates key-person risk |
| **WA concentration** | Entirely dependent on the Perth market — any local economic downturn hits directly |
| **RAC ownership optics** | Some owners may perceive institutional ownership as less personal than boutique competitors |
| **Competitive pressure** | Rezzi, Realmark Strata, and others compete directly in Perth with experienced teams |
| **Margin pressure** | No-commission insurance model and RAC rebate are strong differentiators but reduce revenue per scheme |
| **Talent retention** | Small strata management teams are vulnerable to individual staff departures impacting scheme continuity |

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths

- RAC WA ownership brings exceptional brand trust and a ready-made member acquisition channel
- Technology-forward positioning differentiates from traditional, slower-moving competitors
- No insurance commissions — a genuine, client-friendly differentiator
- Flexible 30-day rolling contracts lower the barrier to switching
- Dedicated strata manager and assistant per scheme — strong service model
- Early adopter and real-world validator of the Town Square platform
- Strong customer reviews citing responsiveness and professionalism

### Weaknesses

- Small team limits scale and creates capacity risk
- WA-only — no national growth pathway currently visible
- Now externally dependent on Town Square (an independent company) for their client portal
- Limited public brand presence outside of Perth and the RAC ecosystem
- Founder-led with limited visible leadership depth beyond Clinton House

### Opportunities

- Perth's growing apartment density creates an expanding addressable market
- RAC member base is a large, underutilised acquisition channel
- Potential to expand service lines (e.g. FM, insurance, legal) under the RAC umbrella
- Deepening the Town Square integration could unlock further efficiency and differentiation
- Growing owner expectations for digital transparency play directly to their strengths

### Threats

- Boutique competitors (Rezzi, others) with strong reputations competing in the same Perth market
- National players (PICA, Bright & Duggan) with deeper resources entering or growing in WA
- If the Town Square relationship were to change, Strata Town loses a core differentiator
- Regulatory changes to the Strata Titles Act WA requiring rapid compliance adaptation
- Economic pressure in Perth property market reducing new development activity and strata formation
