**Date:** April 2026

## What They Do

Town Square is an Australian strata communications and productivity platform purpose-built for body corporate and strata management companies. It replaces fragmented email inboxes with a structured, AI-powered workspace.

**Core product capabilities:**

- **AI-powered case files** — inbound emails are automatically triaged, linked to the correct scheme, lot, and owner, and organised into case files with all related messages, documents, quotes, and approvals
- **Automated workflows** — tasks, priorities, and milestones are assigned and tracked across teams without constant email forwarding
- **Smart email organisation** — AI handles inbox chaos, drafts communications, and surfaces what needs attention
- **Owner & Committee App** — a kanban-style client portal giving lot owners and committee members live visibility over requests, financials, scheme documents, and updates
- **Trust accounting integration** — connects with existing accounting platforms so financial data is accessible without double-handling
- **Reporting & analytics** — real-time dashboards covering workload by team member, message volume by scheme, time-to-resolution, and portfolio heat maps
- **ISO 27001 certified** — enterprise-grade security, Australian data hosting on AWS, compliant with Australian Privacy Principles

Town Square is omni-channel: owners and contractors keep using email — the platform handles the routing and organisation behind the scenes.

## Who Their Customers Are

**Primary customers:** Strata management companies and body corporate management firms across Australia.

**Key users within those firms:**
- Strata managers (primary daily users)
- Assistant managers and admin teams
- Business owners and principals overseeing portfolios

**End beneficiaries the platform also serves:**
- Lot owners — via the Owner App for transparency and self-service
- Committee members — for approvals, updates, and document access
- Contractors — able to interact via standard email

**Geographic focus:** Primarily Australian, with customers across Perth, Brisbane, Sydney, and Newcastle. Expanding nationally.

## Competitors & Market Position

Town Square operates in the Australian proptech / strata technology sector.

| Company | What They Do | Relationship |
|---|---|---|
| **Strata Master** | Trust accounting and back-office platform (MRI Software) | Integration target — widely used by Town Square prospects |
| **StrataMax** | Strata management back-office software (QLD-based) | Indirect competitor in workflow tools |
| **Palace / MRI Software** | Broader property management software | Adjacent — not strata-specific |
| **Urbanise** | Strata/facilities management platform | Competitor in digital transformation |
| **Felix / BuildingLink** | Resident communication and building management | Overlapping on owner communication |
| **Strata Town** | Perth strata management company | Customer — uses Town Square as their client portal |

**Town Square's position:** Communications-first, AI-powered layer that sits above trust accounting. It doesn't replace back-office systems — it organises the chaotic communication layer surrounding them. Strata managers already have accounting software; Town Square fills the gap in structured, transparent owner and contractor communication.

---

## Pain Points

- **Integration dependencies** — prospects using Strata Master want integration before committing; without it, the sales cycle lengthens
- **Trust building at scale** — strata managers are cautious adopters; the industry is relationship-driven and slow to change
- **Feature parity pressure** — must keep shipping as AI and proptech competitors mature
- **Sales resource constraints** — small team covering BD, sales, and client success simultaneously
- **National expansion** — growing beyond WA requires market education and local presence in new states
- **Change management** — getting existing customers to fully adopt new features requires proactive onboarding

---

## Red Flags & Risks

| Risk | Detail |
|---|---|
| **Concentration risk** | Small early customer base — loss of key customers is disproportionately impactful |
| **Integration gaps** | No live Strata Master integration yet — a friction point across a large market segment |
| **Incumbent competition** | Larger players (MRI, Urbanise) have deeper pockets and could replicate features |
| **Founder dependency** | Small founding team creates key-person risk, especially in sales and client relationships |
| **Market readiness** | Australian strata industry is conservative — adoption curves may be slower than projected |
| **AI liability** | Errors in AI-drafted communications could damage trust in a sensitive, regulated context |
| **Data sensitivity** | Strata data is legally and financially sensitive — any privacy failure would be reputationally damaging |

## SWOT Analysis

### Strengths

- Purpose-built for strata — deep domain knowledge baked into the product
- Backed by a credible institutional investor (BetterLabs / RAC WA)
- AI features are live and shipping, not just roadmap promises
- ISO 27001 certified — credible for enterprise and compliance-conscious buyers
- Founded by practitioners — genuine industry empathy and credibility
- Omni-channel, email-first design reduces friction for owner and contractor adoption
- Strong early customer advocacy and testimonials

### Weaknesses

- Small team means resource constraints across sales, product, and client success
- Limited integrations (especially Strata Master) reduce addressable market in the short term
- Brand awareness still building nationally beyond WA
- Pricing and tier structure not yet widely understood by the market

### Opportunities

- Australian strata market growing with increasing apartment and high-density development
- Industry-wide digital transformation is still early — large white space
- AI communication tools are becoming expected, not just nice-to-have
- Integration partnerships with Strata Master and StrataMax could unlock major distribution
- Expansion into commercial and mixed-use strata
- SCA (Strata Community Association) partnerships and events for targeted reach

### Threats

- Larger players entering the communications and CX layer of strata software
- Economic pressure on strata management firms reducing software budgets
- Dependency on a few early customers for revenue and social proof
- Regulatory changes in strata legislation creating compliance complexity
- AI-generated communications may face scrutiny in property and legal contexts
